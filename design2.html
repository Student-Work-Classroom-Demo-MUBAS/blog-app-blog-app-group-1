About Malawi

Malawi, known as "The Warm Heart of Africa," is a landlocked country in southeastern Africa. It is bordered by Zambia to the west, Tanzania to the north and northeast, and Mozambique to the east, south, and southwest.

Despite its small size, Malawi boasts an incredible diversity of landscapes, from the high peaks of Mount Mulanje to the vast waters of Lake Malawi, which covers about one-third of the country's area.

Malawi's people are known for their friendliness and warmth, making it a welcoming destination for travelers from around the world. The country offers a unique blend of natural beauty, wildlife experiences, and cultural immersion.

The name "Malawi" comes from the Maravi, an old name for the Chewa people who inhabit the area. The country gained independence from British colonial rule in 1964 and has since developed its own unique identity while preserving its rich cultural heritage.
